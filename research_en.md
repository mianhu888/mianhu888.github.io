title1: Participated Projects
projects:
  - title: Design and Implementation of an AI-Agent-Driven Personalized Educational Companion Robot for Children
    source: Beijing Normal University Research Achievement Translation and Incubation Project
    period: 2026 - Present
    description: This project develops a low-cost, maintainable, child-friendly, safe, and trustworthy educational service robot prototype and builds an integrated hardware-software-algorithm-content platform. By combining speech recognition, facial expression synthesis, sensor-based interaction, and affective computing, it forms a systematic solution for after-school learning and emotional support. The design embeds AI ethics, safety, and privacy protection standards to ensure sustainable and trustworthy educational use.

  - title: Development of Guidelines for Generative AI Use among K-12 Students
    source: Education Management Information Center, Ministry of Education

  - title: Fox and Field
    image:
      src: /assets/img/work/foxfield.png
      alt: Fox and Field
    period: 2023 - Present
    description: What types of scaffolds can narrow the computational thinking performance gap between experts and novices in programming courses? To explore this question, we designed Fox and Field, a block-based programming game with different types of scaffolding support. We deployed it with undergraduate students from both computer science and non-computer science majors, assessed their computational thinking knowledge, and logged their programming process data.

  - title: Code Beats
    image:
      src: /assets/img/work/codebeats.jpg
      alt: CodeBeats
    period: 2020 - 2025
    description: Making music can be a promising way to learn programming. We teach computational thinking through a curriculum in which students learn to code, manipulate, and create hip-hop beats while experiencing computational processes. As students compose beats, they learn fundamental computer science concepts and skills in an engaging and often seamless way.

  - title: Partner4CS
    image:
      src: /assets/img/work/partner4cs.png
      alt: Partner4CS
    period: 2020 - 2023
    description: This project aimed to establish sustainable, high-quality professional development and support for middle school and high school computer science teachers by forming teams of university faculty and undergraduates in computer science, education, and mathematics education, while drawing on successful components from existing models across the country.
